## Elleray Palm — first appearance and early-insertion points

**Current first appearance: snippet 89** (Meeting Room 5, "the goat is spherical"), with zero prior seeding — no mention, glimpse, or rumor exists before Martin walks John in.

**All appearances:** 89–97; 149–154 (Office Blokes, plants the codes); discussed 105, 124–126, 129; 210–213 (bee-farm phone call), 238 (mention); 241 (mention), 282–289 (drone rescue + knife fight, exits into the In Betweens), 308 (mention); 362, 398 (mentions — his codes prove useless); 587 (implied stirring prisoner), 593–598 (didgeridoo rescue, mock death), 614 (mention); author notes 632, 636.

**Gaps:** absent 155–209, 214–237, 290–586 in person (the 290–586 stretch — over half the book — is bridged only by four mentions). If he moves earlier, the 89–148 gap between his two office visits also softens.

**Natural early-insertion points in existing scenes (all pre-89):**
1. **Snippets 63–66 (Denman and Howe introduced).** The agency is established as the fourth-largest Realist Party ad shop; a line placing the Ministry of Messages among its clients — or Palm's name on a visitor log / meeting-room booking — seeds him at the cheapest possible cost.
2. **Snippets 79–80 (MmmMartin's small talk).** MmmMartin already delivers unsettling patter here; having him trail "a very important MoM contact, mmm, wants *you* specifically" converts an existing beat into a hook and makes John's dread start a full chapter earlier. This is the strongest candidate: same character (Martin) who performs the introduction at 89.
3. **Snippets 67–70 (Dennis, pub invitation, Electric Rulers).** Dennis's office gossip could include a sighting of "the enormous Australian bloke" — comic register already matches.
4. **Snippets 58–61 (Number 72 bus).** Esme Cobbler is already planted here as a stranger John risk-assesses; Palm glimpsed at the Commercial Zone stop (impossible to miss, cork hat) would pair the book's two rebel plants in one scene and pay off doubly at 294–295.
5. **Snippets 52–55 (walk through the In Betweens).** Palm operates from the In Betweens later (289); a distant oversized figure among the Crow graffiti would seed him on John's own anxious ground, though this reads more ominous than comic.

Options 1+2 together give a mention-then-tease escalation into the existing 89 entrance without writing a new scene; option 4 is the best if the author wants Palm physically on-page before the office.

## The Jones→John handover at the midpoint

Jones dies at snippet **256** of 625 prose snippets — almost exactly the 41% mark by snippet count, but the *midpoint of the plot's two acts* (the "PART TWO / ACT 2: THE WALRUS" heading artifact sits inside 256 itself). The appearance data shows the handover working in four measurable ways:

1. **Before 256, Jones out-occupies John.** In snippets 1–160 Jones is on-page for roughly 120 snippets to John's ~95, and she owns the openings of both slices (3–49, 83–88). John's only solo material is commute/office routine; every plot-bearing event (Julie, the mast, Wozzy, the abduction) happens to Jones. The book being "more interested in Jones" is visible in raw coverage, exactly as intended.
2. **The cast swap is abrupt and total.** Jones's supporting cast (the Watch: Roger, Paul Bond, Hannah, Shirley, Naj, Daz, Julie) is killed or dispersed in a single scene (251–256). John's replacement cast (Reza, Delt/Delta, Jemima, Evan, Cesira) all first appear in a 20-snippet window, **220–240** — spun up only ~30 snippets before Jones detonates. The two ensembles never overlap on-page.
3. **John's absence from the death.** John is off-page for the bombing (246–255) and spends 256–261 catatonic in front of it on TV; the narrative refuses him agency at the exact handover point, which is the engine of the "reluctant narrative" — and why Palm's recruitment thread (currently starting at 89) is the *only* pre-midpoint machinery pointing at John. Establishing Palm earlier directly strengthens the reader's sense that a John-story has been quietly loading behind the Jones-story all along.
4. **Jones never actually leaves.** Post-256 she appears — as footage, merchandise, photo walls, the Snake's stolen face, the dream — in **every** remaining slice (269→625), with density *peaking* at the Denunciation (373–439). Structurally the second half is John moving through a world papered with her image; the handover is of agency, not of attention.

Secondary effect: Roger inherits a full co-protagonist thread only after the bombing (261–279 is his longest continuous run in the book), replacing the Jones POV slot in the intercut structure until his harmonisation at 463 reduces him to interludes.
