# Tenets → Gaps → Seed Sentences

The eight things this book is *about*, the exact places where the intent isn't yet on the page, and draft sentences to spark your own.

**How to use this:** these seeds are springboards, not finished prose — aimed at your register but not your voice. The engineering is the *payload* (what the sentence has to carry) and the *placement* (which snippet, whose mouth). You write the final line. Cram accordingly.

Two dosage rules carried over from the audits:
- **LEGIBLE** = you can let readers feel this fully in this book.
- **DENIABLE** = reads as one thing now, pays off in the prequel/book three; must never explain itself.

---

## 0. THE MASTER THEME — Vetko is the fear of death · LEGIBLE

Every other tenet is a branch of this. The aesthetic (frozen nineties, banned music, dead tech) is fully on the page; the *why* — one frightened man refusing to let the world outlive him — is nowhere. This is the one big idea you're allowed to make land.

**Gap A — the motive under the whole dystopia is unstated.** Best placed as narrator lore in the founding-creed stretch (73–77).
> *Seed:* "It was never really about the music. It was about a man who'd found the last good year and couldn't stand to be turned out of it — so he moved the whole country in with him and bricked up the door."

**Gap B — Vetko never says it himself.** One line in the Baker confrontation (546 region), dark-joke register so it's character not thesis.
> *Seed:* "You know what's killed more people than me, Ian? Time. Time's the big one. All I ever did was slow it down for everybody."

**Gap C — Beck's death as the hinge that turns control into war on mortality.** After "taking her name in vain" (546) — grief wearing brand-rage.
> *Seed:* "They don't get to say it. Not one of them. They didn't even know her." — the crack, then back to the storm.

## 1. The dystopia is shit at its job · LEGIBLE

**Gap — the incompetence is shown but never claimed, and never tied to Vetko's exit.** Plant the motive at the blimp (77).
> *Seed:* "Word went round he'd spent three years trying to get one single thing done — just the one — and the country he'd taken in a fortnight beat him with paperwork. So he went up in the blimp and let it all rot."

**Gap — the incompetence briefly undercuts the dread.** Fix by making the threat *arbitrary* not competent, at the sock/scanner beat (342).
> *Seed:* "They don't have to work, love. They just have to go off on somebody." — broken scanners as a lottery of ruin, not a safety net.

---

## 2. The meta layer — Vetko knows · DENIABLE (book-three payoff)

**Gap — his first impossibility (the knife-catch, 610) has no earlier pattern, so it reads as an unmotivated genre break.** Plant it as pub folklore, deniable, in the legend chatter (15).
> *Seed (Jones, tall-tale register):* "They shot him twice on the telly during the Tumult. Live, both barrels. He waved."

**Gap — the broadcast's world-stop reads as hyperbole.** Three words at 450 turn it into a rule.
> *Seed:* after "The whole world stopped for him," add — "It always did."

Keep exactly ONE explicit tell ("Looks like this scene's finished," 611). Soften the second one (557).

## 3. The Raven was Erica / Beck Avon · DENIABLE

**Gap — the two threads never quite touch on a physical detail.** The hair is your cleanest deniable seed (146).
> *Seed:* "…bottle-blonde and wild in the breeze, the dark coming through at the roots like something she'd never quite managed to leave behind."

**Gap — the transfer at her death has zero presence.** One remembered line inside John's grief (179), invisible now, devastating on a reread that knows.
> *Seed:* "Waiters, coppers, muggers — everyone did what Erica said the first time she said it. He never once thought that was strange, until the day it stopped."

---

## 4. John is hiding, not scared · DENIABLE-ish

**Gap — his reluctance reads as grief or cowardice, never tradecraft.** Highest-leverage single line, at 95.
> *Seed:* "Eight years of nothing happening. People think nothing happens on its own. Nothing's the hardest thing there is to keep."

**Gap — his checkpoint fear looks like anxiety, not camouflage.** At 53, shift the object of fear from harm to *the record*.
> *Seed:* "The camera hadn't worked in months. He showed it his pass anyway — the one day you don't is the day someone's watching the tape."

---

## 5. Music carries the friendships · LEGIBLE

**Gap — music is silent at every emotional peak, and the music-vs-Harmony fight is never audible in one room.** The single best place: right after the state's frequency dies (595).
> *Seed:* "Into the silence where the country's frequency had been, Reza thumbed the tape deck on. Something illegal and forty years old and human clicked into the quiet."

**Gap — the violinist reveal (530) explains only Reza's accent.** In the bunk-bed dark (527), let it mean something.
> *Seed:* Reza hums to fill the silence and it isn't a banger — it's something bowed, from before. "My dad, innit," he says, when John doesn't say anything.

---

## 6. IT is a brain in a tank · REWRITE (seeds make it inevitable)

The reveal itself is a scene rewrite (the H'gok material). These seeds make the brain-in-a-jar land as *obvious in hindsight* — best British-bureaucracy jokes in the book.

**Gap — nothing foreshadows it.** At the ThinkTank intro / procurement (277).
> *Seed:* the requisition list — "flour, ketamine, and forty kilos of frozen mackerel (recurring, do not query)."
> *Seed:* "the only department in Whitehall with a budget line for aquarium maintenance, renewed every year, questioned by nobody."

**Gap — Baker's possession has no physical anchor.** At his office (267), the half-Pulpo in the jar.
> *Seed:* a strip of Dymo tape on the glass — "SAMPLE 44. DECEASED. DO NOT WATER." (welds Frequency 44 to the tissue 200 pages early.)

---

## 7. Main-characterness is a real, transferable power · DENIABLE (hints only — one or two, never a system)

**Gap — the rule operates from page one but is never fingerprinted early, so the late payoffs read as retcon.** One deniable crowd-bend beat, pre-Walrus, at the standoff (241–245).
> *Seed (Cesira's POV):* "She noticed, with an annoyance she couldn't place, that every chair in the room had ended up angled towards the hostage — and she didn't remember anyone moving them."

**Gap — Palm reads 90% farce, and his knowing never lands.** At the recognition (97), inspection not admiration.
> *Seed:* "He looked not quite at John but slightly around him, at the space he took up, and nodded — like a man checking a hallmark on silver."

*Dosage warning: pick one, maybe two of these across the whole book. More than that and it stops being weird and starts being a system the reader can solve — which belongs to book three.*

## The one-line priority

If you only crammed one: **0-A or 0-B** — give the whole dystopia its heartbeat. Everything else decorates a spine; that *is* the spine.
