# PREQUEL BIBLE — The Beck Avon & Doug Goode Book

Prequel premise (author's tenets): Vetko Block politically upheaves the country; Beck Avon is the big charisma of the rebel group; Doug Goode is just her boyfriend who shows up sometimes; the reader learns only at that book's end that Beck and Doug become Erica and John Blard. Book three pays off the meta layer (Vetko knows he's in a book; main-characterness is a transferable property that passed from Beck/Erica to John at her death). Snippet numbers (s#) refer to the *Parliament of the Snake* manuscript (`inbox.md`); "note s#" marks the author's own scratch notes (s626–638), which are softer canon than prose.

## 1. HARD CONSTRAINTS — what book 2 has already committed to

## 2. THE TWO PROBLEMS TO SOLVE

### 2a. The timeline pinch
**The math:** Tumult/Cesira's fall ≈ 10 years before book 2 (s75, s541). The Raven "disappeared before the bad shit happened" (s298) — and the Clearings (the obvious "bad shit") start at reign year 2, i.e. ~8 years before book 2. John is verifiably in Zone 42 orbit 9 years before book 2 (s26). Erica dies 8 years before book 2 (s183). So Beck/Erica has **~1–2 years** between vanishing and dying — into which book 2 has already stuffed: flee the Capital, build the Blard identities solid enough for hospitals and death registration, establish a client-facing legal practice with a supportive employer, forgetting/confabulation onset, the car incident, diagnosis, chemo, surgery, recovery, UV5 lockdown, relapse, death. And s528 says John saw her blonde "for years." The arc as written *feels* multi-year. No snippet states a hard year, so nothing contradicts — it pinches.

**Options within existing canon (not mutually exclusive):**
1. **Start the illness before the disappearance.** The early symptoms (forgetting, stories that never happened, clumsiness — s179–180) can begin while she is still the Raven. Strongest version: the illness *is* the reason she disappeared — the "benevolent superhero" walked away because her brain was already betraying her, and only Doug knew. This dissolves most of the pinch (arc becomes ~3 years total), gives the prequel a devastating engine, and makes "she disappeared before the bad shit happened" a bitter irony — she left to die, not to escape.
2. **Read "for years" as two.** Blonde from the flight (~9.5y ago) to death (8y ago) plus the years of only-the-photo since is literally "the only way he'd seen her for years" (s528 is ambiguous between "she was blonde for years" and "the photo is all he's had for years"). Cheap, defensible.
3. **Stretch the soft numbers.** "Ten years" (s75, s541) and "nine years ago" (s26) are all round numbers in character voice; the Tumult can sit at 11–12 years back without touching a line. Buys 1–2 extra years for the illness.
4. **Move "the bad shit" earlier.** If "the bad shit" is the *turn* itself — the election-night betrayal, the first purges, the skies closing (year 0–1) rather than the Clearings (year 2) — she vanishes ~10y ago and has ~2 years, matching option 3.
5. **Pre-built identities.** The Blard papers were prepared *during* the rebel years as an exit plan (a rebel cell would have forgers) — so "build the identities" takes zero time post-flight, and the cover-marriage ("we were sort of together because we had to be," s528) was constructed while Beck was still active. The prequel can show the papers being made long before they're needed.
- **Fixed points that cannot move:** death at −8y (s183); John near Zone 42 at −9y (s26); Vetko's Clearings at reign year 2 and blimp at year 3 (s76–77); the state never learns she died (s337, s546).

### 2b. The face problem
**The problem:** The Realists had "posters, little action figures, pogs" (s73) — the Raven was mass-media famous. Yet Erica lived a client-facing professional life two hours from the Capital, shook hands with prospective clients (s182), sat in state hospitals twice, and was never recognized. The only disguise on the page is hair dye (dark s73 → bottle blonde s146/s528). And s73's "nice dark haired lady" implies the public saw *a woman*, not just a logo. This is the single biggest thing the prequel must square (flagged in raven-erica.md §4).

**Assets already in canon:**
- The founders performed **in costume**: Sergey did rallies "dressed like Iron Man" (s73); the whole persona system was invented theatre ("I came up with that bullshit," s545); the present-day masks are of *faces* for Vetko/Sergey/Baker because those three ruled barefaced afterwards — the Raven never had a ruling era, so her iconography can be mask-era only.
- Jemima states the doctrine: "The people need a figurehead… you've got to be an icon" (s344) — icons are constructed, not candid.
- The erasure (s73) means every archive image was purged ~9 years ago; what survives is graffiti, tattoos, and memory — stylized, not photographic.
- Her civilian look is anti-iconic: "round face," grinning, wild bottle-blonde hair (s146) — Reza wolf-whistles at Erica's photo (s622) and doesn't clock her, in a book where he's steeped in Crow lore.

**Options within existing canon:**
1. **Costumed icon, obscure face (recommended by the evidence).** The Raven appeared masked/winged/hooded; "nice dark haired lady" is remembered because the dark hair spilled from under the cowl — hair was her tell, which is exactly why the dye job is the entire disguise (the s73→s146→s528 chain becomes airtight). The prequel stages the mechanism: crowds adore the Raven; nobody looks twice at Beck Avon in the chip-shop queue.
2. **Merch of the persona, not the person.** The pogs/action figures depict the costume (as an Iron Man toy depicts armor, not Tony Stark). One prequel scene of the merch being commissioned — Beck appalled or amused — pays off s73 directly.
3. **The erasure did the rest.** Whatever true photos existed were criminalized ~9 years ago; the prequel can show the purge order (and who gave it — see Vetko in Open Questions), explaining why by book 2 even die-hard Crows couldn't pick her out of a lineup.
4. **Geography.** Her fame was Capital-centric street-level; Zone 42 is provincial. Combine with 1–3 rather than relying on it.
5. **A proof scene.** The prequel should include at least one beat where someone stares straight at unmasked Beck next to her own poster and sees nothing — establishing for the reader that the disguise premise *works*, so book 2's Erica never reads as a plot hole. (Deniable option: the mantle itself bends attention — people see the icon, never the woman — which quietly foreshadows book 3's rules.)
- **Constraint:** whatever is chosen must keep Vetko's s546 rage coherent — he, who knew her real face, never found her in 9+ years. The disguise has to beat *him* too (or he wasn't truly looking in the right way — a book-3-flavored choice).

## 5. OPEN QUESTIONS — free decisions, to be made deliberately

**Identity & backstory**
1. Beck Avon's real origin — where are the "back home" hills (s146)? (Wales rhymes with Jones's Conwy/Capel Seion homesickness but is unclaimed; Chippenham is established as fake.)
2. Beck's profession/skills before the Tumult — book 2 only requires that she could plausibly become a lawyer under a new name within ~a year (or already was one; a legal background would explain the fast practice).
3. Ages: John is 33 in one note (s637) and 38 in another (s629); "You're young, you'll bounce back" (s180) for her. Fix both characters' birth years now — the teenage-infatuation note (s629) and "dead as long as he'd known her" (note s630) imply they met when Doug was ~17, ~16 years before book 2. Decide whether that math is canon.
4. How Beck and Doug met, and what "showed up" means in "a teenage infatuation that would've petered out if she hadn't a) showed up and b) died" (note s629) — did he adore her from afar first (fan of the Raven? schoolmate?) before she re-entered his life?
5. What Doug actually did in the rebel group ("Not exactly," s535; the forged-papers competence; the slogan knack of s92 — was he the movement's word-man? The Realists' own catchy iconography needs *someone* to have written it).

**The Raven**
6. Her rebel group's name and roster — must reconcile with the s470 fate-list and the Crows' later existence. Were her people the original Crows, or did the Crows form after/because of her disappearance?
7. Why she disappeared — illness (fixes the timeline, §2a option 1), disillusionment at the movement's turn, something Vetko did, something the mantle did, or the time traveller's interference (note s626). Book 2 fixes only "before the bad shit."
8. What exactly "the bad shit" is — the turn itself, the Clearings, the Pulpos-style experiments. This choice pins her disappearance date (§2a option 4).
9. Whether she was ever a *founder-equal* inside the four or the movement's conscience bolted on — s73 lists her fourth and gentlest; s541 lists her among what the people wanted. How close was she to Vetko, Sergey, Baker personally?
10. Who ordered her erasure and why — Vetko protecting her memory from the movement's crimes, Vetko punishing her desertion, or Baker sanitizing the pantheon. Each gives s546 a different flavor; all are open.
11. Did she know about the mantle — was her charisma knowing or innocent? (Book 3's transfer mechanics will inherit whatever the prequel implies.)

**Vetko**
12. Vetko's pre-Tumult history — real name? "Vetko Block" reads constructed; the persona system was his invention (s545), so even his own name is fair game.
13. What Vetko wanted from/with Beck — love, ownership, the mantle itself (main-characterness.md: "Vetko wanted it"), or the only person who ever treated him as real. Governs both s546 and the book-3 time-travel motive.
14. Why Vetko really left at year 3 — boredom, the meta-knowledge dawning, going to *look for her* (the blimp as watchtower), or It's counsel. "Dad went to the shops" (s77) is the public story; the truth is unwritten.
15. "King Killer" (s547) + the beheaded King William statue (s256) — did the monarchy fall in the Tumult, and did Vetko kill a king personally? A whole free set-piece if wanted.
16. How much of Vetko's meta-awareness (knows he's in a book, time-travel plan) is *active* during the prequel era, and how visible the note-s626 time traveller is — including whether the traveller is Vetko himself, since the prequel must show the thread ("apparent in the first one") without detonating book 3.

**Sergey**
17. Sergey Kluvitz's fate — the series' most conspicuous blank (full constraints §1.11). Died? Harmonised? Fed to It? Walked away like the Raven did? Also decide: did Sergey and the Raven have a relationship (the tiger/eaten-by-their-own-tiger pun at s77 is sitting there), and does book 3 need him alive?
18. Whether there was ever a Parliament of the Tiger — the yearly renaming (s78) implies a sequence; deciding the sequence dates Sergey's fall.

**Timeline & world**
19. The Pulpos date — the author's own note says the current placement "doesnt fit in the time line" (s631). Decide: pre-blimp (contradicts Baker-as-cause era) or interregnum (then the "Pulpos accelerated the zoning" note needs zoning to still be rolling out years in). Also whether the Pulpos belong in the prequel at all or stay interregnum backstory.
20. UV5 vs. "No virus, only tentacles and eldritch weirdness" (note s637) — if the central conceit kills the virus, what actually swept the country when Erica relapsed (s181–183)? The prequel/epilogue may have to thread this needle; at minimum, don't contradict either until the author rules.
21. Exact Tumult chronology — how the 22 days (s73), the 99 days (s85), the riots, and the Realist electoral win (s72) interleave. Canon fixes the numbers, not the sequence.
22. Tabuk — where, when, against whom, and whether it appears in the prequel (Roger's two years there, s246, are prequel-era-adjacent; a Roger cameo in Tabuk or the Order Police is free).
23. Whether prequel-era cameos of book-2 cast happen at all (young Roger, Jones in the Tumult crowd s72, Cesira's 99 days from inside, Maurice, Ethel) — each is cheap joy but spends coincidence budget.
24. The golden statue (s85) — worth one scene? Its commissioning is a perfect Cesira beat and it's still standing in book 2.

**Structure & reveal mechanics**
25. Where the prequel ends: at the flight from the Capital, at Erica's death, or at the mantle's arrival in Doug (the transfer must be stageable without contradicting s179–183's trace-free grief — §4 item 12).
26. How the Blard reveal lands at the prequel's end — name reveal only, or the full bridge (hair dye, Chippenham papers, the spare-room shrine being assembled)?
27. Whether Elleray Palm (or his kind) exists on-page in the prequel (§4 item 16) — presence, absence, or one deniable silhouette.
28. Whether Baker's It/ThinkTank has prequel-era roots to show ("the countries only super computer," s77) — the origin of It is unwritten and book 3 may want it held back.
29. The prequel's title-era naming: "Tumultuari" is Cesira's word (s541) and "Parliament of the Dragon… cool fireworks" (s78) dates the naming scheme — decide what the prequel's parliament(s) are called, since the series titles apparently follow them.
