# Tenets → Gaps

The eight things the book is *about*, and the exact moments where the intent isn't on the page yet. Each gap points at one of your own lines so you know the spot. The prose is yours — this just marks the holes.

- **LEGIBLE** = let readers feel this fully in this book.
- **DENIABLE** = reads as one thing now, pays off in the prequel / book three. Under-plant, never explain.

---

## 0. THE MASTER THEME — Vetko is the fear of death · LEGIBLE

Every other tenet is a branch of this. The look is all there; the *why* — one frightened man refusing to let the world outlive him — is nowhere.

- **Gap A — the motive under the whole nineties-freeze is never said.** The spot: the Realists' rise, *"no more scary teenagers with their new music, their new ideas… Bring back the glory days."* Right now it's aesthetic. It needs to be revealed as terror of time.
- **Gap B — Vetko never says it in his own mouth.** The spot: the Control Node, raging at Baker — *"why is there rioting in my Residential Zones… taking her name in vain?"* Somewhere in that rant he could name what the control is actually for.
- **Gap C — Beck's death as the thing that turned control into a war on dying.** The spot: the exact beat after *"taking her name in vain,"* where *"his voice rose… a sudden crashing crest of an unseen tsunami."* That crack is grief. It could show it.

## 1. The dystopia is shit at its job · LEGIBLE

- **Gap A — the incompetence is everywhere but never claimed, and never linked to why Vetko left.** The spot: *"dad went to the shops and never came back. It felt like a blessing."* No reason is given for the blimp. The reason is: he couldn't get anything done.
- **Gap B — broad incompetence briefly makes the regime feel harmless.** The spot: Delta's *"wrap stuff in a sock and them scanners aren't worth shit."* A beat there could make the broken tech *arbitrary menace* — it doesn't have to work, it just has to go off on someone.

---

## 2. The meta layer — Vetko knows · DENIABLE (book-three payoff)

- **Gap A — his first impossible moment has nothing before it.** The spot: on the roof, *"He stopped her hand inches from his heart… You were never that important."* That's the first time he does something inhuman, so it reads as the book breaking its own rules. It needs a much earlier, deniable rumour — best dropped into the Watch's pub-legend chatter (the *"Vetko Block wouldn't have stood for this"* kind of talk).
- **Gap B — the world literally stopping for him reads as hyperbole.** The spot: the broadcast, *"The whole world stopped for him… I think you've forgotten who I am."* A tiny touch could make that a rule, not a flourish.
- **Note:** you've got two "this is a scene/the next bit" winks close together near the end — keep the roof one (*"Looks like this scene's finished"*), soften the other.

---

## 3. The Raven was Erica / Beck Avon · DENIABLE

- **Gap A — the two never touch on a physical detail.** The spot: the shrine photo, *"bottle blonde hair wild in the breeze"* — set against the Raven being *"the nice dark haired lady."* Bottle-blonde = dyed. A detail here (roots, a black feather, anything) quietly welds them without saying it.
- **Gap B — the mantle passing to John at her death leaves no trace.** The spot: the *"I watched her die"* flashback, *"the person that was so much bigger than anyone he'd ever met."* One remembered line about people always doing what she said — invisible now, gut-punch on a reread that knows.

---

## 4. John is hiding, not scared · DENIABLE-ish

- **Gap A — his reluctance reads as grief or cowardice, never as a man staying hidden on purpose.** The spot: the Palm desk scene, *"Nothing had happened, exactly how he liked it."* That line could turn from passive into deliberate — nothing takes work; he's *maintaining* it.
- **Gap B — his checkpoint nerves look like anxiety, not fieldcraft.** The spot: *"it didn't open any doors or gain any sort of reaction from the camera but he felt better for doing it."* Shift the fear from getting hurt to getting *recorded* and it becomes camouflage.

---

## 5. Music carries the friendships · LEGIBLE

- **Gap A — the state controls through sound the whole book, and living music never once wins in the same room.** The spot: *"Terminate Connection Permanently? John pressed enter. The omni present hum stopped."* Into that dead silence, one bit of Reza's illegal music is the whole music-vs-Harmony fight, made audible once.
- **Gap B — the reveal that Reza's dad was a concert violinist explains only his accent.** The spot: *"My dad was a concert violinist. I grew up in Surrey."* In the bunk-bed dark it could mean something — his tapes as everything he's got left of the man.

---

## 6. IT is a brain in a tank · REWRITE (plus tells)

The reveal itself is a scene rewrite (the eldritch / H'gok stuff becomes the Pulpos brain). These just make it land as obvious-in-hindsight.

- **Gap A — nothing foreshadows the brain-in-a-jar.** The spot: the ThinkTank's weekly *"procurement session, flour and ketamine don't buy themselves."* Perfect place for the bureaucratic tell (something that only makes sense later — the thing needs feeding / watering).
- **Gap B — Baker's possession has no physical anchor.** The spot: his office, *"a jar of green fluid… suspended half a Pulpo, stitched back together."* A label on that jar could tie it to Frequency 44 two hundred pages early.

---

## 7. Main-characterness is a real, transferable power · DENIABLE (one or two hints, never a system)

- **Gap A — people bending to John operates from the start but is never flagged, so the late payoffs feel like a retcon.** The spot: the standoff, kidnapped John somehow running the room down to *"Can I pick the music for the drive?"* One beat of someone *noticing* the room bend around him.
- **Gap B — Palm is 90% farce; his knowing never lands.** The spot: *"by crikey, its you, its really you."* Tilt him from star-struck fan to something inspecting you — a pilgrim checking a relic.
- **Dosage:** one, maybe two of these across the whole book. More and the reader can solve it — and that's book three's job.

---

## Priority

If you fill one gap, fill **0** — give the dystopia its heartbeat. Everything else decorates a spine; that *is* the spine.
