# Tenets → Gaps

The eight things this book is *about*, and the exact places where the intent isn't yet on the page. The prose is yours — this just points at the holes so you can write into them and cram them in.

Two dosage rules carried from the audits:
- **LEGIBLE** = you can let readers feel this fully in this book.
- **DENIABLE** = reads as one thing now, pays off in the prequel / book three; must never explain itself. Under-plant, don't over-plant.

---

## 0. THE MASTER THEME — Vetko is the fear of death · LEGIBLE

Every other tenet is a branch of this. The aesthetic (frozen nineties, banned music, dead tech) is fully on the page; the *why* — one frightened man refusing to let the world outlive him — is nowhere.

- **Gap A — the motive under the whole dystopia is unstated.** Best as narrator lore in the founding-creed stretch (73–77).
- **Gap B — Vetko never says it himself.** One line, his mouth, in the Baker confrontation (546 region). Dark-joke register so it's character, not thesis.
- **Gap C — Beck's death as the hinge that turns control into war on mortality.** Right after "taking her name in vain" (546) — grief wearing brand-rage.

---

## 1. The dystopia is shit at its job · LEGIBLE

- **Gap — the incompetence is shown constantly but never *claimed*, and never tied to Vetko's exit.** Plant the motive at the blimp (77).
- **Gap — broad incompetence briefly undercuts the dread.** Fix by making the threat *arbitrary* rather than competent, at the sock/scanner beat (342).

---

## 2. The meta layer — Vetko knows · DENIABLE (book-three payoff)

- **Gap — his first impossibility (the knife-catch, 610) has no earlier pattern**, so it reads as an unmotivated genre break. Plant it as pub folklore in the legend chatter (15).
- **Gap — the broadcast's world-stop reads as hyperbole, not a rule.** Tiny addition at 450.
- **Note:** keep exactly ONE explicit tell (611). Soften the second (557) — currently two too close together.

---

## 3. The Raven was Erica / Beck Avon · DENIABLE

- **Gap — the two threads never touch on a physical detail.** The hair (dark-haired Raven → bottle-blonde Erica) is the cleanest deniable seed (146).
- **Gap — the mantle's transfer at her death has zero presence.** One remembered line inside John's grief (179) — invisible now, loaded on a knowing reread.

---

## 4. John is hiding, not scared · DENIABLE-ish

- **Gap — his reluctance reads as grief or cowardice, never tradecraft.** Highest-leverage single spot: 95 (turn "nothing happened, how he liked it" into deliberate maintenance).
- **Gap — his checkpoint fear looks like anxiety, not camouflage.** At 53, shift the object of fear from harm to *the record / being seen*.

---

## 5. Music carries the friendships · LEGIBLE

- **Gap — music is silent at every emotional peak, and music-vs-Harmony is never audible in one room.** Best single place: right after the state's frequency dies (595).
- **Gap — the violinist reveal (530) explains only Reza's accent.** Make it mean something in the bunk-bed dark (527).

---

## 6. IT is a brain in a tank · REWRITE (plus seeds)

The reveal is a scene rewrite (the H'gok material). The seeds just make the brain-in-a-jar land as obvious-in-hindsight.

- **Gap — nothing foreshadows it.** The ThinkTank intro / procurement list (277) is the natural spot for the bureaucratic tells.
- **Gap — Baker's possession has no physical anchor.** The half-Pulpo jar in his office (267) — a label that welds Frequency 44 to the tissue early.

---

## 7. Main-characterness is a real, transferable power · DENIABLE (hints only — one or two, never a system)

- **Gap — the rule operates from page one but is never fingerprinted early**, so the late payoffs (592, 612, 614) read as retcon. One deniable crowd-bend beat, pre-Walrus, at the standoff (241–245).
- **Gap — Palm reads 90% farce; his *knowing* never lands.** At the recognition (97), tilt him from star-struck to inspecting.
- **Dosage warning:** pick one, maybe two across the whole book. More and it stops being weird and becomes a system the reader can solve — which belongs to book three.

---

## Priority

If you only fill one gap: **0-A or 0-B.** Give the whole dystopia its heartbeat. Everything else decorates a spine; that *is* the spine.
