# Tenet Audit: "John Is Hiding, Not Scared"

**Tenet:** John Blard is a reluctant protagonist because he is HIDING — he fled at the end of the prequel and doesn't want the government to find him. His refusals are camouflage discipline, not cowardice.

## 1. CURRENT READ — what John's reluctance looks like as written

**The dominant early read is grief, wrapped in anxiety comedy.** The manuscript gives readers one complete, authoritative, non-hiding explanation for John's withdrawal, early and in narrator voice:

- **Grief (the explicit explanation).** Snippets 179–183: Erica's death and the narrator's flat diagnosis: "*Eight years ago John Blard retreated into a cocoon, and he didn't want to come out of it*" (183). The spare room is "a dusty shrine to a life he wouldn't talk about" (146). Late-book: "Losing the most important person in the world to me has lead to me losing absolutely everything else" (613). Because this is narrated rather than claimed by John, readers take it as ground truth — it actively crowds out "hiding."
- **Fear (the comic surface).** The "P of D" (Probability of Death) running gag from his first scenes — Jones: "Whats the Probability of Death for today Blard?" (30); recurs at 92, 94, 105, 135, 141, 204, 245, 285, 290–291, 354, 504, 526. Stated refusal logic is bodily: "there isn't a film thats worth me getting shot for" (177), "Being shot is a particular fear of mine yes" (243), "Man with gun. Man with gun. Run" (221), hides behind Evan at loud noises (570). His inner voice frames his guilt over Jones as cowardice: "you were too scared dickhead" (496).
- **Inertia/routine.** The refusal speech to Palm — "I am going to sit here… get up, go to work, go home, repeat" (153–154) — plus "I've done my bit. He could go home now" (440, 487–488) reads as passivity, not tradecraft.
- **Discipline** exists in the text but is never labelled, so readers fold it into the anxiety gag.

Net: the reader panel's "comic coward/everyman" read is the correct read of what's on the page through roughly snippet 250. The hiding material is real but back-loaded and oblique.

## 2. HIDING EVIDENCE — lines that already support "deliberately hiding"

The attention-vs-danger tell is present, mostly unmarked:

- **Performing for the record, not the guard.** John shows his pass to the cameras at an *unoccupied* checkpoint — "it didn't open any doors or gain any sort of reaction from the camera but he felt better for doing it" (53). "Days of his life preparing" rehearsed cover-banter for an interrogation that never comes (60). Legend-maintenance, not fear.
- **Tradecraft monologue.** The walking litanies are route-craft: "Stop, check road, two people, one looking at wall, why, why, moving away, fine" (54–55); bus-seat selection by attack angle and "low demographic for terror" (61); "walk quickly, don't run, no threat" (220); under pursuit: "Duck into doorway, avoid spotlight... head down" (492–493) — checking is his ingrained default.
- **Stated in his own head.** "He'd get to stay in the shadows, safe in the knowledge he'd made his contribution" (212). "Years of keeping your head quite literally down and staying silent" (526). "His sense of self preservation perked its ears up... **echoes of old duties**" (435).
- **Others say it out loud.** Reza: "or whatever it is you're hiding in that past of yours" (292); "What you hiding?" — John changes the subject (316); and: "Thought you'd have packed a bag and done one. **Again, you know**" (528). Reza already knows John has fled before.
- **The psychic scenes are the tenet in plain text.** Rosemary Doris: "A man on the run… someone who keeps their face looking at the floor... **They know who you are John Blard**... Layers within layers. A man who is another man" (335–336); the rainy-day goodbye "alone" and "whats this here you're keeping under here—" interrupted (337). John's reaction is to attention-in-his-head, not danger: "in my head Reza" (338); "she did things to me, saw things she shouldn't" (472). "Chippenham — he was sure he was from Chippenham, **he was last time he checked**" (334) is an identity joke doubling as a tell; Erica's photo shows hills "like back home" (146) — home is unnamed and elsewhere.
- **The hunted-man reflex.** "**after all this time** they were going to find him and they were going to kill him" (496) — only makes sense for a pre-existing fugitive.
- **The powerful recognise him.** Baker: "I had to know who you were... John Blard is no one. Who would have thought such a harmless experiment could have **dug up such a relic**" (592). Vetko, with wonder: "**You're only fucking here**" (612). Neither is available to a coward-everyman reading.
- **Knows too much.** A "Pointless citizen" who rattles off agitator factions — "TCO? Gideons Army?" — and deflects with "I watch a lot of news" (297, 299); twice asserts with odd authority "The Ravens dead" / "she died a long time ago" (298, 585), physically buckling the first time.

## 3. THE COMPETENCE LEAKS — set-up paying off, or inconsistency?

- **People follow when he speaks.** First Walrus broadcast (436–440); Maurice's instant "Well blow me it speaks" (505); the Town Hall speech turning a hostile crowd (516–521); the riot-stopping final address (617–618). **As written these read as accidental comedy, not latent charisma** — nothing before 436 shows John persuasive, so Reza's "every time you open your mouth people want to follow you" (614) lands as the author telling the reader what to have noticed — the retcon feeling the panel flagged. (Nice existing irony: John's Town Hall speech is an anti-hiding speech — "You can't hide from life. Stop being so afraid" (518–520) — but it points at the NIMBYs, not back at John.)
- **Impossible luck.** Reza's "someone always seems to save you... nine lives" (613–615) is **half set up already**: Elleray Palm extracts Reza's explicit promise — "**Promise me you'll protect Blard**" (288) — and Palm does two deus-ex rescues (282, 593–596). The "luck" is partly engineered guardianship. But 288 is played as a gag and never recalled, so readers don't connect it.
- **Palm choosing him.** The strongest existing scaffold: "Ellerays asked for you specifically" (91), "He's asked for me, by name" (125), "You were here all along... its you, its really you" (96–97), "thats why I gave you those codes mate I knew" (285), through to Cesira's "a man with no motivation but to go home had usurped her at every key moment" (524). Palm functions exactly as intended — the narrative comes for John regardless. **The gap:** John never once wonders *why him*. He treats Palm as random weather. One beat of John suspecting Palm knows who he is would convert the whole Palm thread from absurdist harassment into dread.

**Verdict:** the leaks are all present and mostly late; none contradict, but only Palm's thread is structurally load-bearing. The charisma has zero set-up, the luck has hidden set-up (288) that isn't surfaced, and the recognition beats (592, 612) pay off a secret the early book never priced in.

## 4. HARDENING SUGGESTIONS — early sentence-level tilts from "scared" to "hiding"

All preserve the comedy and reveal nothing of the prequel:

1. **Snippet 53 — make the dead camera the point.** After "he felt better for doing it," add one clause shifting the object of fear from harm to record: the camera hadn't worked in months *and he showed it his pass anyway, because the one day you don't is the one day somebody's watching the tape.* Converts checkpoint compliance from neurosis to camouflage discipline on John's first full page.

2. **Snippets 54–55 — one witness-count beat inside the threat litany.** The litany counts ways to die; insert a single beat counting ways to be *seen*: "*Nobody had looked at him twice. Good. Two people had seen his face today; average was four.*" One sentence re-keys the whole recurring gag from coward to a man keeping a tally only fugitives keep.

3. **Snippet 95 — turn passivity into maintenance.** "Nothing had happened, exactly how he liked it" is the thesis line of the coward read. Extend it: "*Eight years of nothing happening. People assume nothing happens by itself. Nothing takes work.*" The cheapest, highest-leverage single edit in the book — retro-tints every refusal after it as practice, not paralysis.

4. **Snippet 125 — a go-bag beat after "He's asked for me, by name."** Add a wordless action after Jones leaves: John checks something he never explains — a zipped bag at the bottom of the wardrobe, cash taped somewhere, ten minutes at the dark window. No dialogue, no backstory. Pre-loads Reza's "packed a bag and done one. **Again**" (528) so it detonates instead of puzzling.

5. **Snippet 178 — let Jones's question go pointedly unanswered.** She asks the tenet's own question: "Whats so special about you? Whys it gonna be you who catches a bullet." Sharpen John's deflection so it's visible — he starts answering too precisely ("*Nobody catches a bullet by accident, they catch it because someone went looking—*") and stops himself, changes subject to the wine. Readers register a question John declined to answer, so Doris's "man on the run" (335) confirms a suspicion rather than introducing one. Matching micro-edit in 177: "worth me getting shot for" → "worth being *seen* for."

6. **Snippet 146 — one wrong note in the shrine.** Give Jones one detail that doesn't add up: no wedding photo, no second person in any picture, a background that could be anywhere, the frame's cardboard back worn from being packed and unpacked. Grief stays real; the reader learns the grief has redacted edges.

**Optional structural note:** the charisma leak needs one pre-Walrus foreshadow — the earliest natural slot is the Big Deal standoff (241–243): one reaction line (Cesira or Delta noting, sourly, that everyone had stopped pointing things at him and started listening) makes the Town Hall and Reza's 614 read as payoff. And surfacing Palm's protection-promise once — Reza muttering "made a promise, didn't I" during the hospital rescue (497) — converts "impossibly lucky" into "guarded, and he doesn't know it."
