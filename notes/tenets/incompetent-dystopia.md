# Tenet Audit: "The Dystopia Is Shit at Its Job"

**Tenet:** The regime is deliberately bad at being a dystopia — rubbish drones, too much bureaucracy, very British, not very successful. Part of why Vetko leaves is he can't get anything done.

## 1. ON THE PAGE — where the regime's incompetence is dramatized

The tenet is already *everywhere* — this is the most consistently executed idea in the book. Grouped by system:

**Wozzy and the Mark 3 drones**
- **45** — Wozzy introduced as "an imperfect rusty sphere," dented, graffitied "WOZ E," it "bounced a couple of times and jittered spasmodically."
- **46** — damaged voice control: "a difficult electronic puberty."
- **47** — armed but wobbly: "stuttered a little in flight before righting itself"; Jones is more baffled than scared ("Since when have zone Drones had weapons").
- **49** — enforcement is just petty theft: "a big buzzy money stealing bee."
- **103** — "the Drone audibly calculated, its gears whirring."
- **104** — "Poor useless Wozzy," bullied in the imagined "Drone playground"; nearly slashes Jones by accident while escorting her "FOR YOUR SAFETY."
- **160-161** — the gnome-as-weapon mitigation review; "The Drone hovered, which wasn't particularly useful"; "You really are a shit Drone Wozzy. Wozzy failed to drop out of the sky. It was worth a try."
- **39** — surveillance has known holes: "Drone won't come anywhere near this hill."
- **199** — even the regime's own kidnappers exploit "the Dead Zone."
- **407, 406** — the Chapedrone/Pete: drone maintenance axed by "budgetary restructuring, out with bad rubbish, rubbish like me"; "No budget for Petes."
- **497, 543, 547** — drones "couldn't keep up with a genuine Mustang," "flittered ineffectually above the hordes, their weapons not readied," "buzzed after them in vain."

**Checkpoints and scanners**
- **54** — RZ42's turnstile "had broken some months ago… When it comes to crime and terrorists, its the thought that counts."
- **55** — the checkpoint is "the bastard child of biological security and unambitious budgets."
- **56** — one guard "wielding an AR-15 rifle and a thermos full of tea."
- **59** — the security bus still shows "the very faint outline of the Megabus mascot."
- **64** — the Commercial District checkpoint "had yet to permit any scimitar wielding murderers" (damning with faint praise).
- **138** — the thesis in miniature: the scanner "needed replacing… but theres budgets and austerity and local councils don't really exist anymore so its take what you can get. It still worked didn't it?"
- **215-218** — the swab-test checkpoint: results take "two minutes… known to be slower… Sometimes its inconclusive… Sometimes it never comes back, just goes missing in the ether."
- **302-303** — cardboard permits work because "Checkpoints were all shirt and no shoes"; and the single best statement of the tenet in the book: *"Trust a former Prime Minister to plan around government ineptitude, the illusion of control might keep many in line but its a different story when you've seen its inner workings. Theres a reason you never see the attendants having a go on the funfair rides, they've seen all the missing screws"* (303).
- **342** — "wrap stuff in a sock and them scanners aren't worth shit."
- **352** — station "slated for demolition and to be filled in with concrete but you know, what with budgets how they are, a fence will surely do."
- **384** — "We're plumbers" plus a waved wrench beats a Denunciation-night checkpoint.

**Ministries**
- **274-276** — "Even the Ministry of Peace had a budget"; mind-control-by-baked-goods "budget had been cut after the obesity crisis"; strategy set by "budgets and what the boss fancies."
- **277** — the Think Tank runs the drones, but the permit system? "No thats the Post Office." The military? "After Tabuk? God no."
- **387-391** — the MoM infiltration: unmanned turnstiles with "no power running to it," a lifeless temperature scanner, Sue drinking wine on the desk — "Its all broken my girl, been on the fritz for weeks, give me your name and I shall stick it in The Book"; "the securities off for the move anyway"; "canteen loos have been out of order for weeks."
- **425** — national message control derailed by biscuits: "how can we control the flow of messages across the nation with low blood sugar?"
- **10, 16** — the Constabulary via the Neighbourhood Watch: drone reports say nothing's wrong while a woman disappears; the Watch's "special monthly call out used up."
- **367** — a Constabulary literally "running a dog fighting ring."
- **453** — a pensioner voice-overrides a drone: "Drone 896, override peace protocols."
- **507-509** — Felicity's speech (the explicit thesis): "There aren't enough Drones. The Prime Ministers mood will be impacted."
- **1** — even the opening image: "hastily patched holes in the roof a monument to local governance and budget realities."

**Designed or accidental?** The evidence strongly reads as *designed*: the budget/austerity vocabulary recurs like a motif (1, 54-55, 138, 274-276, 352, 406-407, 508), and snippet 303 states the funfair-missing-screws thesis outright. The problem is **placement, not consistency**: the two explicit statements of intent (303 and 508) both arrive in the back half. In Act One the incompetence is shown but never *claimed*, so a reader can spend 300 snippets unsure whether the wobbliness is worldbuilding or sloppiness — exactly the reader-panel complaint.

## 2. VETKO'S MOTIVE — how close is the text to the tenet?

**What's there:**
- **77** — the departure itself: "Vetko disappeared off to his Blimp three years in, just did one, dad went to the shops and never came back. It felt like a blessing." **No reason is given.**
- **85** — the blimp as state failure: "retirement blimp. Biggest waste of tax money since Cesira Brogue built herself a golden statue."
- **531** — his return line: "Hello Ian. This is a fuck up isn't it."
- **545** — his only parting instruction was "Dont. Fuck it. Up" — governance delegated in three words.
- **546** — the closest the text comes to the tenet: "Ian, why is there rioting in my Residential Zones? Ian, why have you been pissing about with sound waves while **my country is falling apart**?" Plus "Nothing is about anything but me. All you had to do was not lose control."
- **547** — his self-image is conqueror, not administrator: "I'm the God Butcher, the King Killer, Ghengis Fucking Can."
- **611** — he leaves *again* the moment governing resumes being the job: "Looks like this scenes finished… Lucky fellas."

**What's missing:** the causal link. As written, Vetko left for no stated reason and returns angry that things broke. Nothing anywhere says he left *because the machinery of state was too broken/bureaucratic for him to get anything done*. The character logic is fully compatible with the tenet (bored warlord, no patience for administration, exits the moment the "scene" ends), and 546's fury at "pissing about… while my country is falling apart" is one clause away from it — but a reader cannot currently deduce that the incompetence *drove him to the blimp*. Snippet 77 is the natural place to plant it and currently plants nothing.

## 3. CONTRADICTIONS — where the regime is scarily competent

**Fine (competence as marked exception):**
- **Vetko himself** — 450 (his voice alone freezes a riot), 555-557 (beats Baker with the sonic device), 610. The man is terrifying; his state is not. This *supports* the tenet.
- **Five Eyes vs Mark 3s** — 205 ("gleaming black metal and angles"), 300, 440-442 (the methodical office-tower hunt), 530. This two-tier system is *explicitly reconciled* at 508: "The Five Eyes are fine but the rest of the Drones haven't had a firmware update since the Prime Minister ascended." Consistent — but the reconciling line comes ~80% in.
- **Denunciation-night saturation security** (324, 373-376) — already half-lampshaded at 342: "Half the Drones in the CZ are floating round Parliament Square anyway," i.e. competence-by-concentration that strips the rest of the country bare. Good, but the lampshade is one throwaway line spoken *before* the reader sees the spectacle.

**Genuine friction points:**
- **459** — "The drones have been overridden to shoot on sight, sir. There won't be any more prisoners." A regime that can flip its whole fleet to lethal on command reads more capable than the same fleet that "flittered ineffectually" 80 snippets later (543) and dies to one EMP (562). Needs a beat of cost or malfunction.
- **Roger's psychoacoustic conditioning** (506, 554, 611) — genuinely works and is genuinely horrifying. Mostly reconciled: Felicity calls the programme "years of failed mind control attempts" (508) and Baker presents Roger as the one "perfect working soldier" (554) — n=1 out of a nation. Keep it that way; don't add a second success.
- **The abduction of Jones** (199-200) — the regime's most effective act in the book, but note it's two goons with a pipe exploiting a Dead Zone ("Matthews stop hitting everything with a pipe"), so it stays on-tenet: the state's real weapon is thuggery, not the machine.
- **454** — Production Zone shock-whips and 16-hour shifts: brutal, but brutality-as-substitute-for-competence; arguably on-tenet, just unlampshaded.

## 4. HARDENING SUGGESTIONS — small, phone-editable, in existing scenes

1. **Snippet 77 — give the blimp a motive (the single highest-value edit).** After "Vetko disappeared off to his Blimp three years in, just did one, dad went to the shops and never came back," add one rumour-flavoured sentence in the narrator's register, e.g.: *"Word was he'd spent those three years trying to get one single thing done — one — and the country he'd conquered in a fortnight defeated him with forms."* This makes the tenet legible in Act One and makes 546 land as payoff instead of surprise.

2. **Snippet 546 — close the loop in Vetko's rant.** He already says "my country is falling apart." Add one clause of self-implication, e.g. after "All you had to do was not lose control": *"You think I went up there for the view? You can take a country in a week, Ian. Try getting anything to bloody work in one."* Now his departure, his return, and his final exit (611) are one coherent character.

3. **Snippet 342 — turn the sock line into the stakes-fix.** The reader panel's worry ("incompetence undercuts surveillance stakes") is best answered by making the *threat arbitrary rather than competent*. After "them scanners aren't worth shit," give Delta (or the narrator) one beat: *"They don't have to work. They just have to ruin whoever they go off on."* This reframes every broken scanner as a lottery of punishment — dread preserved, incompetence intact. (The material already supports this: Wozzy's random £67 fine at 160, the £18,000 fine at 414.)

4. **Snippet 374 — lampshade the Denunciation exception at the point of spectacle.** After "tens of temporary checkpoints and Drone scanners were set up to police all comers," add a clause: *"every functioning drone south of Birmingham in one square mile, which was one way of making the rest of the country invisible."* This converts the book's scariest security set-piece from "inconsistency" into visible triage, and pre-echoes Felicity's 508 speech.

5. **Snippet 459 — price the shoot-on-sight order.** Keep the chill, add the rot in Felicity's same breath: *"The drones have been overridden to shoot on sight, sir. The ones that still take the override."* One clause keeps the regime's cruelty intact while confirming even its atrocities run on failing firmware — the exact tone of 508 ("intentions, rather than results," 509).

**Bottom line:** the manuscript doesn't need more incompetence — it has it in abundance and (via 303 and 508) even states the doctrine. It needs the doctrine *declared early and tied to Vetko*: plant intent at 77, echo menace-through-arbitrariness at 342, lampshade the two big competence spikes (374, 459), and pay it off in Vetko's own mouth at 546. Five sentence-level edits and the reader-panel "inconsistency" becomes the book's visible spine.
